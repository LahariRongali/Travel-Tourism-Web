The Gateway of India, built in 1924, stands as Mumbai's most iconic landmark overlooking the Arabian Sea. This magnificent arch-monument commemorates the 1911 visit of King George V and Queen Mary. Blending Hindu and Muslim architectural styles, it serves as a popular tourist destination and starting point for Elephanta Caves tours. The monument represents Mumbai's historical significance and continues to be a symbol of the city's maritime heritage.

Delhi

üèõÔ∏è War Memorial

India Gate, the 42-meter tall war memorial in New Delhi, honors Indian soldiers who sacrificed their lives in World War I. Built in 1931, this architectural marvel draws inspiration from Paris's Arc de Triomphe. The eternal flame, Amar Jawan Jyoti, burns beneath the arch, commemorating unknown soldiers. Surrounded by verdant lawns, it's a beloved evening destination for families and tourists alike.

Indore

üëë Royal Palace

Rajwada Palace, constructed in 1747, exemplifies the grandeur of Maratha architecture in Indore. This seven-story marvel combines Maratha, Mughal, and French architectural elements. Featuring intricate wooden carvings, detailed paintings, and a magnificent courtyard, the palace sits at the heart of Indore's bustling market district. It stands as a proud symbol of the Holkar dynasty's royal heritage.
