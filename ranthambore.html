Welcome to Ranthambore

Where ancient fortresses meet wild tigers, in the heart of Rajasthan's royal wilderness.

In the land where royalty once roamed, now the tigers reign supreme, amid lakes and ruins that tell tales of both natural and human history.

Discover Ranthambore

Ranthambore National Park, set against the backdrop of the ancient Aravalli and Vindhya mountain ranges, is one of India's most celebrated tiger reserves. This former hunting ground of the Maharajas of Jaipur now stands as a testament to wildlife conservation.

Beyond its famous tigers, the park is a unique blend of natural and historical heritage, featuring the magnificent 10th-century Ranthambore Fort, stunning lakes, and diverse wildlife. The deciduous forests and open grasslands create perfect viewing conditions for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.
