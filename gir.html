Welcome to Gir National Park

Home of the majestic Asiatic Lion and Gujarat's crown jewel of wildlife conservation.

In the heart of Saurashtra lies Gir, where the roar of the Asiatic lion echoes through time, a testament to India's successful wildlife conservation story.

Discover Gir National Park

Gir National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary, also known as Sasan Gir, is the last remaining natural habitat of the Asiatic Lion in the world. This magnificent sanctuary spans across 1,412 square kilometers of deciduous forest, grasslands, and rocky hills.

Beyond its famous lions, Gir hosts a diverse ecosystem with over 2,375 distinct species of fauna. The sanctuary's unique landscape of teak forests and savanna grasslands provides the perfect environment for its varied wildlife to thrive, making it a crucial biodiversity hotspot in India.

Must-Visit Places in Gir National park

Asiatic Lion

★★★★★
(5,291 reviews)

The pride of Gir, the Asiatic Lion is smaller than its African cousin but no less majestic. With a population of over 600 lions, Gir offers the unique opportunity to witness these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat. Best spotted during early morning or late afternoon safaris.

Indian Leopard

★★★★½
(3,888 reviews)

The elusive Indian Leopard is one of Gir's most skilled predators. These graceful cats can often be spotted in the deciduous forests and rocky outcrops. Known for their adaptability, they share their territory with lions, making Gir one of the few places where these big cats coexist.

Sambar Deer

★★★★★
(4,136 reviews)

The Sambar Deer, India's largest deer species, plays a crucial role in Gir's ecosystem. These majestic herbivores can be frequently spotted near water bodies and in the teak forests. Their presence is vital for maintaining the predator-prey balance in the sanctuary.
