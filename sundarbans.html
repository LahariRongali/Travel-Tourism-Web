Welcome to Sundarbans

The world's largest mangrove forest and home to the magnificent Royal Bengal Tiger.

Where the land meets the sea, the Sundarbans stand as nature's shield, protecting life both above and below the waters, a UNESCO World Heritage site that tells the story of survival and adaptation.

Discover Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, spanning across India and Bangladesh, is the world's largest mangrove forest ecosystem. This unique delta region covers approximately 10,000 square kilometers and is formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers.

Beyond its famous Royal Bengal Tigers, the Sundarbans is a complex ecosystem hosting numerous species of flora and fauna. The intricate network of waterways, mudflats, and small islands creates a perfect environment for diverse wildlife, making it one of the most significant biodiversity hotspots in South Asia.

Must-Visit Places in Sundarbans

Sajnekhali Wildlife Sanctuary

★★★★★
(4,891 reviews)

The gateway to Sundarbans, Sajnekhali Wildlife Sanctuary houses an impressive mangrove interpretation center, a turtle rearing center, and a museum. It's the perfect starting point for exploring the diverse ecosystem of the Sundarbans.

Dobanki Watch Tower

★★★★½
(3,567 reviews)

Famous for its canopy walk, Dobanki Watch Tower offers a unique perspective of the mangrove forest from above. The elevated walkway provides excellent opportunities for bird watching and spotting wildlife in their natural habitat.

Netidhopani Watch Tower

★★★★★
(4,223 reviews)

Home to the historic ruins of a 400-year-old temple, Netidhopani Watch Tower offers spectacular views of the surrounding mangrove forest. It's an excellent location for wildlife photography and viewing the stunning Sundarbans sunset.
